Fitted: A Responsive Web App

A UI Design Case Study

February 2019-April 2019

Overview

Fitted is a responsive web app aimed to motivate individuals into a fitness routine that fits their fitness level, schedule and interests. This project was completed for CareerFoundry’s UI for UX Designers course.

Purpose and Context

Fitted is a project I worked on for CareerFoundry’s UI for UX course. I was required to create a responsive web app based on certain feature requirements, brand and style guidelines, key messaging, and user stories. This project had a duration of 3 months.

Objective

Motivate people into an exercise routine that suits their level, schedule, and interests.

The 5 W's of Fitted

Who?

People who are new or returning to fitness, want to find activities they like and get into a good routine.

What?

A responsive web app for users to search and view routines, guides, daily challenges and other information on any device.

When?

The app can be used whenever they like. They will use it while they are searching for, scheduling and following routines.

Where?

When on the go or when practicing exercise routines at home, in the park, on the street, etc. Users can stay fit anywhere, as long as they're logged in on a device.

Why?

To become healthy and enjoy the associated benefits; exercise should be fun and suited to each user; to save time by fitting exercise into daily routines.

Moodboard

I started by creating 2 different Moodboards to communicate the visual directions for my web app. Moodboard 2 was chosen due to the vibrant colors and overall energy it presented. The idea is to evoke energy and positivity into the user, and the bright and vibrant colors would be more beneficial in comparison to more subtle colors.

Wireframing

A mobile-first approach was taken before designing the tablet and desktop screens. This was to ensure that I didn’t overindulge in the information presented, which would ultimately lead to difficulty in designing the mobile screens in the long run. After the basic screens were down, I was able to then create my color palette and typography.

Final Mockups

Landing Page

The landing page is simple for mobile. It provides a short, clear description of Fitted, and an option to sign up or log in.

Onboarding

The onboarding screens ask information from the user about their goals and health data to provide more customized results for their suggested workouts. The user is able to skip this process if they wish, and can always modify their data later.

Home Screen and Daily Challenge

The home screen provides exercise options based on your needs and goals, and keep track of your progress. The daily challenge is just another push to get the user to work out every day, even if only for 5 minutes.

Beginning Your Workout

Beginning your workout is an easy process. You can select your desired workout and view details to be certain this is the right workout for you. You have the option to either schedule your workout for a later date, or begin right away. If you choose to begin your workout, you are able to follow along with the videos to be sure you are doing the workout correctly.

Your Profile

Your profile is a place to view all your goals, from your steps per day, workouts, weight loss/gain journey and awards. The purpose of this page is to help you stay on track of your daily exercise goals, and see how far you've come.

Create a Custom Workout

Have a different workout in mind? We have a solution. Create your own custom workout to add to your routine.

Conclusion

Throughout this course, I learned the importance of implementing UI fundamentals correctly in order to create a more fulfilling experience for the user. By evoking certain colors, typography and imagery, it helps create a more enjoyable experience by setting the tone right from the start.
